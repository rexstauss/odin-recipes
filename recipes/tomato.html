Air Fryer Roasted Tomato Soup

PREP TIME: 15 min; COOK TIME: 35 min
Makes about 3 servings

Description

This Air Fryer Roasted Tomato Soup is made by roasting fresh,
summer tomatoes, peppers, and aromatics in the air fryer. The
roasted vegetables are then blended into a smooth, creamy
soup with heavy cream, parmesan cheese, balsamic vinegar,
and fresh basil. It’s a simple, flavorful soup that’s perfect for a
light lunch or dinner for two.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound fresh tomatoes
  • 1 small white or sweet onion
  • 1 red bell pepper (about 8 ounces, medium)
  • 1 medium carrot, scrubbed clean
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • Avocado oil or extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 and 1/2 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 teaspoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 tablespoon grated Parmesan cheese
  • 6 basil leaves
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream

Steps

Prep the veggies

  1. Slice the tomatoes in half.
  2. Slice the onion into quarters or sixths.
  3. Cut the pepper into 2" pieces.
  4. Slice the carrot in half lengthwise, then
    into 1" chunks.
    (Carrots take longer to cook than the other
    veggies, so the pieces need to be small in
    order to finish at the same time as the rest.)
  5. Peel the garlic cloves (you can leave them whole).
  6. Toss the veggies in a small amount of oil. You
    don't need much; just a drizzle will do.

Roast the veggies

  1. Preheat your air fryer to 370ºF, then cook for 25 minutes.
  2. Flip and rearrange the veggies at the 12-minute mark using tongs.
    (Don't shake the basket; remember there are liquids exuded from
    the veggies in the basket.)

Smooth out the soup using a high-speed blender

Peeling the tomatoes and peppers is optional if you have a high-powered blender.

  1. Add the roasted veggies to the blender. Pour in enough chicken
    broth to cover the veggies, and add the vinegar, Parmesan
    cheese, sugar, and basil leaves. Add a big pinch of salt and
    black pepper.
  2. Blend on high for 30 seconds, or until the soup is at your
    preferred consistency.
  3. Pour the soup into a soup pot or Dutch oven set over medium-high heat.
  4. Skip to the "Finish the soup" step.

Or, using an immersion blender

  1. If you’re using an immersion blender instead of an upright blender, add the
    roasted veggies from the air fryer to a soup pot or Dutch oven set over
    medium-high heat. Add 1 cup of chicken broth to start, along with the vinegar,
    Parmesan cheese, sugar, and basil leaves (if they’re large, tear them into smaller
    pieces). Run the immersion blender through the pot, pulsing until it reaches your
    desired consistency. If the soup is too thick, add more chicken broth 1/4 cup at a time.

Finish the soup

  1. Let the soup heat through for 5 to 10 minutes, then turn off
    the heat and let rest for a few minutes.
  2. Stir in the heavy cream. Taste, and add more salt and pepper as needed.
